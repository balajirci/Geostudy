Key field tools

N
Compass and back bearing
Back bearing = bearing + 180 degrees. Subtract 360 if over 360. Used to check the return direction from a waypoint.
P
Pacing distance
Your step is approx 0.75 to 0.8 m. Count double-steps (left foot down = 1). Distance = steps x 0.75 m.
W
Wind direction
Observe bent trees, cloud movement, smoke drift. Wind direction = where wind comes FROM, not where it goes.
H
Estimating height
45 degree method: walk back until angle to top = 45 degrees. Your distance from base equals the object height. Shadow method: compare your shadow to the object shadow ratio.

Question types to practice

Choropleth map
Explain one limitation of this choropleth map.
Key points: choropleth maps hide variation within regions, assume uniform distribution, and can mislead due to large-area / small-population zones (e.g., Northern Canada looks dominant visually).

Dependency ratio
DR = ( (0-14) + (65+) ) / (15-64) x 100
A DR of 50 means 50 dependants per 100 working-age people. Canada's is approx 47. Aging communities (e.g., rural Nova Scotia) can exceed 70+.
Urban vs. rural temperature
Why might temperature data from the rural station differ from the urban station?
Urban: concrete and asphalt absorb heat, reduced evapotranspiration, waste heat from vehicles and buildings, reduced wind.
Rural: vegetation cools via transpiration, higher albedo from soil and crops, no anthropogenic heat sources.

Population patterns

Fastest growing: Alberta, B.C., Ontario. Driven by immigration, energy sector (AB), and tech/finance (ON/BC).
Aging fastest: Atlantic provinces (NB, NS, PEI, NL). Out-migration of youth, low immigration, natural decrease.
Ecumene: Approx 90% of Canadians live within 160 km of the US border, due to climate, agriculture, and trade access.

Climate types: one key feature each

Pacific
Mild, wet winters. Orographic precipitation on coast. Smallest annual temperature range in Canada.
Continental
Extreme temperature range (down to -40 C in winter, hot summers). Low precipitation, mostly summer convective rain.
Atlantic
Moderate but variable. Influenced by Gulf Stream (mild) and Labrador Current (cold/foggy). High year-round precipitation.
Arctic
Permafrost, very low precipitation (technically a desert), extreme cold. Short cool summers only.

Economic geography

Resource towns: Fort McMurray (oil sands), Sudbury (nickel), Kitimat (aluminum). Boom/bust cycles, in-migration of workers, male-skewed demographics.
Service hubs: Moncton (logistics, bilingual services), Kelowna (tourism/retirement), Waterloo (tech/education). More diversified and stable.
Command and control cities: Toronto, Montreal, Vancouver. Corporate HQs, finance, culture, immigration gateways.

Essential calculations

Gradient / slope angle
Gradient = vertical rise / horizontal distance
As ratio: 1:n (e.g., 1:10 is steep)
On a topo map: contour interval x contour lines crossed = rise. Map distance x scale = horizontal run.
Dependency ratio
DR = ( youth(0-14) + elderly(65+) ) / working(15-64) x 100
Population density
Density = total population / area (km squared)
Natural increase rate
NIR = crude birth rate - crude death rate (per 1,000)
Compass back bearing
Back bearing = bearing + 180 (if over 360, subtract 360)
Height estimation (45 degree method)
Height = your distance from base (when angle to top = 45 degrees)
